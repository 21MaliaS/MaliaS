More About the Sun

Img 5


The shockwave from a supernova caused the cloud of interstellar dust to collapse and form a solar nebula. When the cloud collapsed it formed protostars, which is just a star in its early stages of development. When fusion began, the stellar blasts created a wind that caused the cloud to stop pulling matter into it, allowing it to stabilize and no longer collapse in on itself. Using the Core Accretion model to explain this, once the cloud collapsed, the material began to spin, which casued the gravity to strenghten and heat up the center of the disk (the sun). Eventually, at the center of the disk, where it was hotest,the hydrogen atoms began to combine and create helium. As the young sun formed and began to heat the disk it was created in, the gas within it began to evaporate, leaving the forming planets less time to absorb it, which prevented them from becoming stars. What additionally prevented the early planets from becoming stars was that the sun consumed more than 99% of the original matter within the disk. The other clumps of matter that eventually did become planets did not have enough mass for a gravitational collapse to occur and start nuclear fusion. Additionally, because of physics and something in particular, called angular momentum, the earth will not collide with the sun. Since the earth has its own motion and speed it orbits the sun with, the speed it goes around the sun would need to change in order for the sun's huge mass and gravitational pull to make the earth collide with the sun.
